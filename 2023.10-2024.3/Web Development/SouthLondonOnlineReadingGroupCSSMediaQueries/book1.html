The Design of Everyday Things by Don Norman

The Design of Everyday Things is a best-selling book by cognitive scientist and usability engineer Donald Norman about how design serves as the communication between object and user.

Current Discussion: What feelings did this book evoke for you?

A particular question from the book chapter

Jonh Smith

I highly recommend reading this to avoid daft and lasting mistakes. Told with examples and anecdotes, this book should be a must read for anyone involved in any product design aspect.
30 October 2018

Emily Watson

This is my first time reading this book. And I have to say I am really enjoying it. It is obviously based on the authors most loved interest. Norman shows that he is an ordinary person who gets frustrated with (bad) design in the same way we all do.
28 October 2018
